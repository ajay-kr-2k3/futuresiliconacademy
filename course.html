Static Timing Analysis (STA)

Think of a relay race‚Äîeach runner must pass the baton at the right time. If someone is too slow or too early, the entire team fails.
In chip design, STA (Static Timing Analysis) acts like the timing referee. It ensures every signal (data) moves correctly from one flip-flop to the next:
ÔÇ∑ Not too late ‚Üí Avoid Setup Violation
ÔÇ∑ Not too early ‚Üí Avoid Hold Violation

Why Do We Need STA?

‚úÖ Ensures the chip works correctly at its target frequency.
‚úÖ Detects timing problems without slow gate-level simulations.
‚úÖ Helps improve logic placement for better performance.
‚úÖ Prevents expensive chip re-spins after fabrication.

*STA ensures that your chip behaves reliably in real hardware, chip doesn't fail when power is ON.
For every VLSI Engineer:
‚ÄúUnderstanding STA is not optional‚Ä¶It's the heart of making high-performance and reliable chips.‚Äù

EMIR / PDN

ÔÇß EMIR analysis = Electromigration + IR Drop analysis ‚Üí Used to ensure power integrity and long-term chip reliability.
ÔÇß The Power Delivery Network in a chip is the complete wiring system that delivers power (VDD) and ground (VSS) to every cell, macro, and block in the design.

It ensures that:
ÔÇ∑ Each transistor receives correct supply voltage
ÔÇ∑ Power delivery is stable across chip
ÔÇ∑ Variations in power do not disturb timing or logic state
